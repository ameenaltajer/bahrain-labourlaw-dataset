Article 120

A government office called the "Labour Case Management Office" within the Ministry of Justice. This office is responsible for preparing labor cases for court hearings. The office is headed by a Chief who is a judge of the High Civil Court and supervises the work of the office. The office also has a sufficient number of members who are judges of the Small Civil Court. The head and members of the office are appointed by a decision of the Supreme Judicial Council, and enough employees are also attached to the office. The Minister of Justice is responsible for issuing decisions on how the office should work and determining the procedures for preparing labor cases and notifying the litigants.
