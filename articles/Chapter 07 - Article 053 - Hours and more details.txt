Article 53

a) Subject to the provisions of Paragraph (a) of Article (51) of this law, the worker may not be actually employed for more than eight hours per day, unless otherwise agreed upon, provided that the workerâ€™s actual work hours do not exceed ten hours daily.

b) The working hours and rest periods shall be organized so that the period from the beginning of the working hours to the end thereof shall not exceed more than eleven hours per day, and the rest period shall be calculated from the hours of presence if the worker is during it at the workplace.

c) Excluded from the provision of the previous paragraph are workers who engage in intermittent work by nature, which is determined by a decision of the Minister, provided that the period of their presence does not exceed twelve hours per day.
