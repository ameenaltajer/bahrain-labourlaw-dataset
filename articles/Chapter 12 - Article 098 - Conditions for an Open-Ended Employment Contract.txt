Article 98

An employment contract is considered open-ended if:

1) If the contract is concluded without specifying its term.
2) If the contract is concluded for a period exceeding five years.
3) If the duration of the original and renewed contract exceeds five years.
4) If the parties to the fixed-term contract continue to implement it after its expiration without an explicit agreement to renew it.
5) If the employment contract is concluded to perform a specific job, and it takes more than five years to complete.
6) If the employment contract for a specific job is renewed, and the period of completion of the original job and the jobs for which the contract was renewed exceed five years.
7) If the employment contract for a specific job ends, and the parties continue to implement it after completing this job without an explicit agreement to renew it."
